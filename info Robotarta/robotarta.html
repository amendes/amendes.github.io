ROBOTARTA

The Robotarta was my most crucial college assignment. I started this research to study coral reefs close to my natal city of Joao Pessoa in Brazil. The idea was to build a robot capable of disguising itself as a sea turtle not to disturb the environment from which it would record videos.

The design, inspired by the Naro Tartaruga <"http://www.naro.ethz.ch/p2/tartaruga.html"> , was very efficient in facilitating the manufacturing process. It would also serve as a skeleton for future and different species of sea turtles.

The first year of research was mainly to study the movement and species and gather the most information about sea turtles. The design in 3D cad built the Solidworks software helped generate simulations later. The materials we used were mostly Polyvinyl chloride (PVC).

Some of my responsibilities were teaching other students computer-aided design, testing the mechanical properties of the parts, listing and acquiring all the materials for the next year to start making the robot.

In 2015, we started the second part of the project Azul. The build. With all the tools and knowledge of mechatronics, I began to manufacture the parts needed for the project. I started working on the electronics and simulations of the robot as well.

We had a complete build of the Robotarta to show by the end of the year. We presented it at the college science fair called CIENTEC. It was pretty impressive to have something so big built using various engineering techniques.

The following year we worked on an international article for the most significant Mechanical Congress of Latin America, the COBEM. But, we also ran simulations, designed the robot's electronics, and started to make the robot move.

By the end of the project, we had a working robot. However, some of the mechanical elements were incompatible and broke down during the testing. The lack of funds during academic research is frustrating, but all the knowledge and experience were very fruitful.
